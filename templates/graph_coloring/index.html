How It Works

Graph Coloring is the assignment of labels (colors) to vertices of a graph such that no two adjacent vertices share the same color. The minimum number of colors needed is called the chromatic number.


Welsh-Powell Algorithm:

  1. Sort vertices in descending order by degree
  2. Assign the first available color to each vertex
  3. Ensure no adjacent vertices share the same color
